# Guardian Forensic Review Standard

## 1. Purpose

This standard defines how Guardian incidents, drills, failures, security decisions, and certification evidence must be forensically reviewed.

A Guardian security claim is not accepted unless forensic evidence proves what happened, why it happened, what Guardian decided, what Guardian executed, and what the final result was.

---

## 2. Global Rules

- Forensic review must be evidence-based.
- Unknowns must be documented as unknowns.
- Assumptions must not be treated as facts.
- Every critical incident must have a timeline.
- Every certification drill must have forensic review.
- Missing evidence must be treated as certification risk.
